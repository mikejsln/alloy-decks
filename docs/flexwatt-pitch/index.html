Problem

Industrial plants overpay for power and waste their flexibility

  • Demand/potencia charges are 30-70% of a heavy C&I electricity bill (Briggs & Stratton, 2024) [needs verification on regional %]
  • Idle gensets and shiftable processes sit dormant — no plant builds the orchestration layer alone
  • No single plant is a market-relevant resource; flexibility is stranded value
  • No LatAm-native software both cuts the bill today and pools flexibility for grid revenue tomorrow

Why now

The model is proven globally — LatAm is the open lane

  • Sympower raised ~EUR42M Series B, manages ~2.7 GW of flexibility
  • Enel X (9+ GW) and Voltus (~8.1 GW) run large C&I demand-response globally — none LatAm-native at scale
  • Guatemala's PEG-5 tender drew 51 bidders offering ~4.7 GW vs 1,400 MW sought — capacity tension is real
  • Central American markets (MER/SIEPAC) are maturing; Demanda Interrumpible already exists as a live revenue product
03 / 12
Solution

Two stacked jobs: cut the bill now, earn grid revenue later

  • Job 1 — Cut my bill (live today, regulation-independent): forecast demand-charge exposure, dispatch load-shift + gensets, shave the peak ~40%, no capex
  • Job 2 — Pay me to be flexible (regulation-gated upside): pool flexibility across plants, monetize via Demanda Interrumpible, graduate to ancillary/balancing revenue
  • Cost-avoidance today, grid revenue tomorrow — the base case stands on Job 1 alone
  • Software-only orchestration over existing meters, gensets, and loads

Product

Software-only orchestration on the studio stack

  • Demand-charge forecast + M&V baseline turns a meeting into a paid, verifiable savings number
  • Shadow-mode dispatch (human-executed, no automated equipment control) proves savings are real and production-safe

  • Claude design library + plain HTML/CSS/JS + Supabase + GitHub Pages; English default, Spanish toggle

Market

Path to >=$500M TAM via four named vectors

  • Blended thesis ~$0.8-1.8B; clears the $500M floor on geography alone, without the regulation-gated revenue leg
  • (1) Geography: CA wedge (~0.4-0.9 GW flexible) → Mexico → Brazil/Andean/Southern Cone (8-14 GW LatAm flexible MW)
  • (2) More jobs per customer: cost-avoidance → DR fleet → ancillary/capacity bidding (regulation-gated hypothesis)
  • (3) More customer types: plants → data centers, agribusiness, cold-chain, EV depots, co-located BESS
  • (4) New products on the flexibility dataset: forecasting, procurement/hedging, Scope-2 MRV

Business model

Price against the bill, not the seat

  • Phase 1: platform fee per connected site + 15-25% savings-share on verified demand-charge reduction [% needs verification vs LatAm ESCO/M&V norms]
  • Phase 2: take-rate on dispatched-MW market revenue (regulation-gated — not underwritten in base case)
  • Phase 3: data products (forecasting, hedging, Scope-2 MRV) — high-margin lines on a sunk dataset
  • Per-site ACV ~$225K/yr (5 MW × ~$45K/MW captured, set below Voltus's disclosed $98K-$279K/MW-yr)
  • Software-only → ~75-80% gross margin at scale, no inventory or working-capital drag

Competition & novelty

Validated model, empty LatAm-native lane

  • Global incumbents (Enel X, Sympower, Voltus, GridBeyond) own the model — only the LatAm geography is fresh
  • Novelty = LatAm-native fast-follower; the durable, transferable moat is proprietary LatAm industrial-flexibility data + the assembled pool
  • Software commoditizes; speed-to-pool is the defense — the dataset compounds with each plant added
  • Data network effect: more plants → better archetype forecasts → easier to win plant N+1
  • Risk: a single Enel X LatAm move could close the lane — time-boxed, so move fast
